### Main Content

#### Mistake #1: The Defensive Slouch (30-90 seconds)
**[Scene 3: Demonstration of poor posture]**
"Mistake number one - the defensive slouch. When you hunch your shoulders, cross your arms, or lean back like you're trying to disappear, you're broadcasting insecurity. Women interpret this as low confidence and disinterest."

**[Scene 4: Demonstration of confident posture]**
"Instead, plant both feet firmly on the ground, shoulders back, chest open. Take up space like you belong there. This signals confidence and shows you're engaged in the conversation."

#### Mistake #2: Avoiding Eye Contact (90-150 seconds)
**[Scene 5: Wrong - looking away, at phone, around room]**
"Mistake two - the wandering eyes. Looking at your phone, scanning the room, or staring at the table tells her you'd rather be anywhere else."

**[Scene 6: Right - appropriate eye contact]**
"Maintain eye contact for 3-5 seconds at a time, then briefly look away and return. This creates connection without being intense. Eye contact shows you're present and interested in what she's saying."

#### Mistake #3: Fidgeting and Nervous Energy (150-210 seconds)
**[Scene 7: Examples of fidgeting behaviors]**
"Number three - the nervous fidget. Tapping fingers, bouncing legs, playing with your hair or touching your face constantly. This screams anxiety and makes her feel uncomfortable too."

**[Scene 8: Calm, controlled movements]**
"Keep your movements slow and deliberate. Rest your hands comfortably, control your leg movement, and use purposeful gestures when you speak. Calm energy is attractive energy."

#### Mistake #4: Invading Personal Space (210-270 seconds)
**[Scene 9: Demonstration of space invasion]**
"Mistake four - the space invader. Leaning in too close, touching too soon, or positioning yourself where she feels trapped signals desperation and lack of social awareness."

**[Scene 10: Appropriate spacing and positioning]**
"Respect her personal bubble - about arm's length until she shows signs of comfort. Sit at an angle rather than directly facing her, which feels less confrontational and more relaxed."

#### Mistake #5: Closed-Off Hand Positions (270-330 seconds)
**[Scene 11: Examples of closed body language]**
"Number five - hidden hands. Keeping your hands in your pockets, crossed arms, or clenched fists creates barriers between you and signals you're hiding something."

**[Scene 12: Open, confident hand positions]**
"Keep your hands visible and use open palm gestures when speaking. This builds trust and shows you're genuine. Rest them comfortably on the table or your lap when listening."

### Transformation Summary (330-390 seconds)
**[Scene 13: Before and after comparison]**
"Let's see the transformation. Before: slouched, avoiding eye contact, fidgeting, invading space, hands hidden. After: confident posture, appropriate eye contact, calm energy, respectful spacing, open gestures."

### Call to Action (390-420 seconds)
**[Scene 14: Direct to camera]**
